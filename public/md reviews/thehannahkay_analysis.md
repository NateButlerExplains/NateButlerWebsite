## 🎨 Design Patterns & Best Practices to Note

### 1. **Clear Service Segmentation**
The site segments keynote speaker services by **audience type**:
- Women's leadership
- Corporate/business
- Virtual/remote
- Healthcare/medical

**Why it works:** Allows SEO optimization for each segment + shows specialization

### 2. **URL Structure**
```
/keynote-speaker/{segment}/
```
- Hierarchical and semantic
- Clearly shows content relationship
- Good for SEO (category → subcategory)

### 3. **Consistent Page Titles**
Pattern: "[Offering] - Hannah Kay Herdlinger"
- Includes keyword (keynote speaker)
- Includes personal brand
- SEO-optimized

### 4. **Minimal Navigation**
Clean, simple main menu with:
- Home
- Keynote Speaker sections
- About
- Contact
- Downloads

**Why it works:** Reduces decision fatigue, focuses on conversion

### 5. **Dual Call-to-Action Strategy**
- **Contact page** for booking inquiries
- **Download materials** for research/consideration

### 6. **Strategic Downloads**
Makes high-value content available (media kit, bio) to encourage sharing + builds authority

## 💡 Key Elements for Your Site

If building something similar, consider:

### ✅ Must-Have Sections
1. **Homepage/Hero** - Clear value proposition
2. **Service pages** - Segment by customer type or use case
3. **About** - Build credibility and personal connection
4. **Contact** - Easy booking/inquiry method
5. **Resources/Downloads** - Lead magnets, media materials

### ✅ URL Strategy
- Use hierarchical structure: `/service/segment/`
- Make URLs semantic (human-readable, keyword-rich)
- Keep structure consistent across pages

### ✅ Content Length
- Homepage: 5-6KB
- Service pages: 6-10KB
- Support pages: 1-5KB
- Keep it scannable, not overwhelming

### ✅ Specialization Signals
- Multiple pages targeting **different audiences**
- Shows expertise in specific niches
- Improves SEO for diverse search queries
- Makes site more useful to different visitor types
